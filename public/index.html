ℹ️ How the scoreboard works

Every ticker the Screener and Momentum sections surface is logged once a day with its entry price. The numbers below are realized forward returns pulled from actual price history — nothing is simulated.

Reading a card

  • Expectancy badge (Positive / Negative / Pending) — the headline verdict, based on the 1-month average return (falls back to 1-week, then 3-month until it matures).
  • 1-Week / 1-Month / 3-Month — average return, win rate, and sample size (n) for each horizon.
  • Avg win / Avg loss — typical size of winners vs losers.

How it's counted

  • Each signal is scored on its first appearance, so names that linger for days aren't over-counted.
  • Strong Sell returns are inverted — positive means the short worked (price fell).
  • Treat small samples (n under ~15–20) as noise, not signal.

Turning signals off

  • Click ✓ Enabled / ✕ Disabled to hide a tier from its section (e.g. hide "Setup" from the Screener). It keeps being tracked so you can re-enable it if it recovers. Saved on this device only.

When numbers appear

  • Tracking is forward-only — it can't backfill picks from before it was switched on.
  • 1-Week fills after ~5 trading days, 1-Month after ~21, 3-Month after ~63. Until then a horizon reads "pending."
  • Picks are logged automatically each weekday. To force a snapshot now: /api/tracker?op=track&force=1.

⚡ CERN — forced-flow events

A different bet from every other tab. The screeners hunt momentum; CERN hunts forced flow — moments when someone has to trade regardless of price (a fund dumping a deleted index name, tax-loss selling in December, a margin spiral). Forced selling overshoots, then reverts. CERN measures the dislocation, checks the flow is uninformed (no news/earnings/attention behind it), and learns how much reverts.

⭐ This is a learning system first, a trading system second. It logs and resolves every qualifying event whether it trades or not — so it learns from 100% of the world while risking capital on a fraction. Months 0–3 will be mostly LOG_ONLY / PROBE (paper). That isn't it failing — that's the archive filling and the model earning its confidence.

🎯 The three decision types

  • TRADE — passed every gate (completion, absorption, posterior P(profit)≥65%, ship-gate) and is sized with ¼-Kelly. Rare early.
  • PROBE — a paper position the engine takes to learn a promising-but-uncertain event type (Thompson-sampled exploration budget).
  • LOG_ONLY — logged and resolved for learning, no position. Watching these resolve is how you learn to trust (or distrust) it.

🧠 The posteriors panel

Each event type shows κ (kappa) — the fraction of the dislocation it has learned reverts — with its uncertainty (±sd) and the number of resolved events (n). High κ + low sd + high n = a trusted edge. A drifted type (struck through) means the world changed and the engine auto-shrank its sizing until the evidence re-tightens. Candidate alerts are recurring unexplained reversions the engine wants you to name as new event types.

🚫 What to keep in mind

  • Real size is gated behind a posterior t-stat ≥ 2 per event type — it will not bet big on a hunch.
  • All seven types are live: margin spirals + tax-loss selling (auto-detected from price/volume), plus index add/delete, lockup expiries, fund fire-sales and sell-side downgrades (calendar/flow/ratings feeds).
  • Fire-sales & downgrades are proxies (honest): real ETF creation/redemption flow isn't on our data tier, so a fund's heavy-volume drawdown stands in for redemptions; and an analyst downgrade is a softer, part-sentiment "forced flow." In both cases the gates + counterfactual logging sort the signal from the noise.
  • Two data gaps (honest): attention is proxied by a volume spike, and estimate-revisions aren't wired yet — so the "uninformed" check is good but not perfect.

The moat is the dataset this builds, not any single trade. Let it log, watch the κ posteriors move, and trust it only once they've earned it.

📘 Start Here — A Beginner's Guide

· how to use this app to find stock picks, in plain English
New to investing? Read this first. This app is a research dashboard — it scans the market and ranks stocks using data-driven signals so you don't have to stare at charts all day. It is not a crystal ball, a tip service, or financial advice. Used well, it helps you find better-than-random candidates and avoid obvious mistakes. That's it — and that's still valuable. Below is the simplest way to use it.
🧭 4 honest truths before you risk a single dollar
1. No signal is a sure thing. The best edges here are modest — they tilt the odds slightly in your favor, like a casino's house edge, not a winning lottery ticket. You win by making many small, sensible bets, not one big "sure" one.
2. The market's "weather" matters more than any single pick. When the overall market is falling, almost everything goes down with it. Avoiding bad conditions is the most powerful thing you can do.
3. Only invest money you can afford to lose. Never rent money, emergency savings, or money you need within a few years.
4. Practice first. "Paper trade" (track picks on paper or in a free simulator) for a few months before using real money. See if it actually works for you.
🌦️ Step 1 — Check the market "weather" (the regime)
Look for the regime indicator (shown on the 📓 Edge Book and Markets tabs). Think of it as a traffic light:
🟢 Risk-on = green light. The market is healthy; it's a reasonable time to look for stocks to buy.
🟡 Neutral = yellow light. Proceed carefully, smaller bets.
🔴 Risk-off = red light. The market is stressed. The app's own research shows buying here usually loses — the smartest move is mostly to stay out and wait. This one rule is the single biggest thing separating good results from bad.
📝 Step 2 — Get your shortlist
The easiest starting point is the 📓 Edge Book → "Sleeve A · Conviction longs." That's a short, ranked list of the strongest names the app sees right now (already filtered by the green/yellow/red light above). Start there.

Want to explore more? Other tabs are just different "lenses" on the market:
• 📊 Markets — stocks breaking out to new highs with strength.
• 🧠 Momentum — names ranked by trend and quality.
• 👻 Ghost Accumulation — quiet stocks that may be getting bought before a big move.
How to read any of them: most tabs sort names into tiers (or give a score). Higher tier / higher score = stronger signal. Start at the top of the list, never the bottom.
🤝 Step 3 — Prefer agreement (this is the secret)
The highest-quality picks are the ones that show up as strong in more than one place. If a stock is top-ranked in the Edge Book and shows up in Ghost Accumulation and has insiders buying — that agreement is far more meaningful than any one signal alone. When several independent signals point at the same name, that's your best setup. When they disagree, it's usually best to skip it.
🛡️ Step 4 — Protect your money (the part most beginners skip)
• Spread it out. Don't put everything in one stock. ~10–15 names means one bad pick won't sink you.
• Size each bet small. A common rule: risk no more than 1–2% of your total on any single stock.
• Decide your exit before you buy. Know the price where you'll admit you were wrong and sell. Small losses are survivable; big ones aren't.
• Don't chase. If you missed it, let it go — there's always another setup. FOMO (fear of missing out) is expensive.
🏆 Step 5 — Keep the app honest
Visit the 🏆 Scoreboard tab. It tracks how the app's past picks actually performed — win rates and returns by signal. Before you trust any signal with real money, check whether it has actually been working. If a section's track record is weak, weight it less. This app is built to be held accountable — use that.
📖 Plain-English dictionary
Ticker — a stock's short code (Apple = AAPL).
Momentum — a stock that's already trending up; the app leans on the idea that strength tends to continue (for a while).
Regime — the overall market mood (risk-on / neutral / risk-off). The "weather."
Conviction score — the app's combined 0–100 rating of how strong a stock looks. Higher = stronger.
Tier — a quality bucket (e.g. top tier vs watchlist). Higher tier = stronger signal.
Long — betting a stock goes up (the normal "buy"). Short — betting it goes down (advanced; skip as a beginner).
Diversify — spread money across many stocks so no single one can ruin you.
Stop-loss — a pre-decided price where you sell to cap a loss.
Paper trading — practicing with pretend money to test a strategy risk-free.
⚠️ Important: This app is an educational research tool, not financial advice, and nothing here is a recommendation to buy or sell. The signals carry only modest, uncertain edges, and past performance never guarantees future results. Investing involves real risk of loss. Do your own research and consider speaking with a licensed financial advisor before investing real money.

How to get the most out of this app

A momentum & market-regime dashboard. Used honestly, it's a strong co-pilot for your decisions — not an autopilot, and not a money printer. Here's how to actually benefit.

⭐ The one rule that matters most: respect the regime badge at the top of this tab. Risk-On = hunt. Neutral = be selective, size down. Risk-Off = stand down on new long trades — this is the clearest, most repeatable finding in all our back-testing.

⏱ The 2-minute daily routine

  1. Check the regime badge first. If it's red, the best move today is usually not trading.
  2. Glance at Markets → Sector Rotation to see where money is flowing in vs. out; lean toward leading sectors.
  3. Only then look at individual names.

🧭 What each tab is for

  • 🧠 Custom Screener — your workspace. Apex / Loaded tiers are a shortlist of strong names to research, not buy signals. Read the pillar bars to see why a name scored. Use the 📊 Portfolio panel for disciplined position sizing & concentration warnings.
  • 📊 Markets — Breakout Screener (idea generation), Sector Rotation (where money flows), Sectors (today's leaders), Backtest (expectations).
  • 🏆 Scoreboard — your reality check. Open it weekly; let it (not your memory) tell you which signals are actually working, and disable the ones that aren't.
  • 🔥 Alerts — real-time momentum flips; good for timing a name you already like.
  • ⭐ Picks · ⚡ Options · 📰 News — context to understand a name, not to decide on one.

🚫 What not to do (lessons the back-testing paid for)

  • Don't take new long trades in Risk-Off.
  • Don't treat "Apex" as a guaranteed winner — it's a strong candidate in a good tape, nothing more.
  • Don't over-concentrate; heed the sector-concentration warning.
  • Don't bet big expecting alpha — the app has no proven standalone edge, so size with humility.
  • Don't go unhedged into a downturn just because tight stops sometimes whipsaw.

Stay on the right side of the regime, size with discipline, and let the Scoreboard keep you honest. That's how you benefit from this app.

👻 Ghost Accumulation — how to use it

Ghost looks for the opposite of the Momentum tab. Where Apex hunts confirmed breakouts, Ghost hunts names being quietly accumulated before the move — rising relative strength on heavy buying, a tightening supply vacuum, and (the new lever) insider buying.

⭐ Same one rule: respect the regime badge. Risk-On = these accumulation setups are worth hunting. Risk-Off = the kill switch downgrades every tier a notch and you should stand down on new longs — accumulation signals fail in ugly tapes too.

🧠 Momentum vs 👻 Ghost — when to use which

Both tabs read the same universe, and a strong name in a good tape will often show up in both. The simplest way to hold the difference in your head — it's about where on the timeline you're catching a move:

🧠 Momentum
"Is this breakout worth buying now?"
  • Fires after the breakout confirms
  • Requires a confirmed setup + no weak pillar
  • Pure price / volume / fundamentals
👻 Ghost
"Is something being bought before it breaks out?"
  • Fires earlier — no breakout required
  • Needs breadth (≥3 strong pillars)
  • Adds insider buying — data the chart can't show

Where they diverge — the names worth noticing:

  • Insider cluster-buying but middling momentum → Ghost flags it, Momentum ignores it.
  • Mid-accumulation, not yet broken out → Ghost Stalking, Momentum shows nothing.
  • Clean breakout that already ran → Momentum Loaded, Ghost rates it lower (the quiet accumulation is already spent).

Honest note: because both lean on relative strength and share the same candidate pool, expect meaningful overlap. The real value of Ghost is the earlier timing and the insider signal — and whether that timing edge is real is exactly what the pick ledger is being built to measure.

🎯 How to read a Ghost card

  • Score /100 & tier — 👻 Ghost = score ≥80 with ≥3 strong pillars (broad accumulation); 🥷 Stalking = ≥65, ≥2 strong (building); 👁 Watch = ≥50 (early footprints). Higher tier = more pillars agree, not "more likely to go up."
  • The 6 pillar bars tell you why it scored — read them, don't just trust the number.
  • The 🏛 Insider line is the signal Ghost adds over every other tab: real open-market insider buys/sells over 90 days, with a cluster-buy flag when multiple insiders are buying. A green net with a cluster flag is the strongest version.
  • Entry / Stop / Target come from the same level engine as the other tabs — a starting framework, not a mandate.

🧱 The six pillars

  • ① Relative strength — quietly outperforming the index. The most validated factor.
  • ② Accumulation footprint — up-days-on-volume; buyers in control.
  • ③ Accumulation vacuum — tight, coiled base on drying volume. Deliberately weighted lowest — our own research found this kind of base/dry-up signal has ~zero edge, so don't lean on it.
  • ④ Smart-money flow — up/down volume + volatility-adjusted momentum.
  • ⑤ Catalyst overlay — real revenue/EPS acceleration + narrative + an earnings catalyst on the horizon.
  • ⑥ Insider buying — the new, still-unproven lever. Promising, not gospel.

🚫 What to keep in mind

  • It re-ranks the breakout pool, not the whole market (yet). Ghost scores the same candidates the screener surfaces, through an accumulation lens. On a quiet large-cap day it can legitimately show nothing — switch the scope to Small caps, or just check back.
  • The weights are static priors. The self-learning engine that tunes them is a later phase; it won't even activate until ~40 of these picks have resolved (≈2–3 months). Until then, treat Ghost as a hypothesis generator.
  • Insider buying is informational. It's the reason Ghost exists, but it has not yet been validated on this app's data — size accordingly.
  • Everything from the main guide still applies: respect the regime, size with discipline, let the Scoreboard keep you honest.

Use Ghost to find names being bought before they move, then confirm the story yourself. It's an early-warning radar, not a buy button.
