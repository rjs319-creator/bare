# Experiment #3 — Insider Conviction Incremental Value (2026-07)

**Question.** Does NSL Engine 2 (opportunistic-insider conviction, SEC Form 4) add forward
predictive value **orthogonal to price momentum**, on real free data?

**Status:** Experimental / inconclusive

## Method
- Universe: 56 liquid large/mid-caps (56 confirmed SEC filers).
- Baseline: 6–1 price momentum (126-bar lookback, 5-bar skip), point-in-time.
- Signal: opportunistic-insider conviction as-of, masked by **Form 4 filing date** (no look-ahead).
- Label: real next-open fill held 21 sessions (purged; unelapsed dates dropped).
- Panel: 17 non-overlapping monthly decision dates (2025-01-23 → 2026-05-27).
- Test: date-clustered rank-IC of baseline / augmented / signal-alone / **baseline-orthogonal** signal,
  Bonferroni over 3 specification variants (`lib/nsl/incremental.js`).

## Panel diagnostics
- Samples: **409**  ·  non-zero insider readings: **354**  ·  used dates: **12**
- Dropped — no momentum history: 0, not-yet-a-filer: 543, label unelapsed: 0

## Result

| Model | Forward rank-IC |
|---|---|
| Baseline (momentum) | IC -0.011, t -0.19, n 12 |
| Augmented (momentum + insider) | IC -0.0451, t -0.81, n 12 |
| Insider alone | IC 0.0156, t 0.2, n 12 |
| **Insider ⟂ momentum (incremental)** | IC 0.0267, t 0.35, n 12 |

- ΔIC (augmented − baseline): **-0.0341**
- Incremental significant after Bonferroni (t ≥ 2): **false**
- **Verdict:** `inconclusive` → recommendation `observe`

## Honest reading
The decisive quantity is the **baseline-orthogonal** insider IC. It is weakly positive (IC 0.0267) but far from significant (t 0.35, needs ≥ 2), so nothing is promotable — but it is not clearly zero either. The correct action is to OBSERVE: log it prospectively and revisit as the sample grows, not weight it.

## Known limitations (not code-solvable on the free/Starter stack)
- Universe is **current survivors** (no delisted names) — survivorship-unsafe; a production verdict needs a PIT security master.
- Form 4 parse is bounded to 60 recent filings/name; deep-history insiders may be undercounted.
- Insider readings are **sparse** — most cross-sectional cells are 0, so the IC is driven by the few active names each month.
- Monthly labels are ~non-overlapping but adjacent; residual autocorrelation is possible.
